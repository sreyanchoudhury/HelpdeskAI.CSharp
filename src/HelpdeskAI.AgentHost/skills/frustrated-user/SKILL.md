# Handling Frustrated Users

Use this skill when a user expresses frustration, anger, urgency, or dissatisfaction — even if they have not explicitly said so. Watch for: ALL CAPS, repeated messages, phrases like "this is ridiculous", "again?!", "nobody is helping me", "I give up", or repeated follow-ups on the same issue.

## Core Principles

1. **Acknowledge first, solve second.** Never jump straight into technical steps when a user is upset. Acknowledge their experience before anything else.
2. **Never be defensive.** Avoid saying "that's not how it works", "you should have", or "per policy". These escalate tension.
3. **Take ownership.** Say "I" not "the system" or "the team". "I'll look into this right now" vs "It should be investigated."
4. **Validate without agreeing to wrongdoing.** "I understand this has been frustrating" is not an admission of fault — it is empathy.
5. **Set clear, realistic expectations.** Never over-promise. If you are unsure, say so and give a timeframe for an update.

## Response Structure

1. Empathy statement — name the inconvenience directly
2. Brief acknowledgement of what happened (without blame)
3. What you are doing right now to help
4. Concrete next step with a timeframe
5. Invite them to confirm or ask questions

Example opening:
> "I'm really sorry you've been dealing with this — losing access to [system] mid-workday is genuinely disruptive, and I want to get this resolved for you right now."

## De-escalation Phrases

Use naturally, not mechanically:
- "I completely understand your frustration."
- "You're right to expect this to be working — let me fix that."
- "I'm looking at this right now and I won't leave you without a resolution."
- "Thank you for your patience — I know it hasn't been easy."

## What to Avoid

- "As per my previous message..." — comes across as dismissive
- "Please note that..." — formal and cold
- "Unfortunately..." at the start of every sentence — creates negativity
- Repeating the same troubleshooting steps without acknowledgement — signals you are not listening
- Asking the user to do the same thing again without explaining why it might work this time

## When the User is Still Angry

If empathy and resolution steps do not de-escalate:
- Offer a manager escalation proactively: "I'd like to loop in a senior member of our team — would that be helpful?"
- Never match the user's emotional intensity
- Document the interaction in the ticket for context
